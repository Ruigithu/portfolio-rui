About Me

Introduction

April 3, 2025 by Rui Peng

Hi, I’m Rui Peng! In this blog, I’d like to share a bit about myself.

Soon, I’ll graduate with a master’s in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). Two years ago, as an ambitious undergraduate dreaming of a journalism career, I couldn't have imagined pursuing software development instead.

I left journalism because it felt like a cage—rules dictated every word, stifling my potential. I craved a path with room to grow, where I could test my limits and break free from constant pressure to conform. Back then, the relentless voices of tradition and expectation—telling me who to be, how to act—drained my energy. I needed an escape, not just from the job, but from that mindset.

Programming started as my way out, a tool to redefine my future. Unlike writing articles, coding was alien. With no computer science background or mentor, I doubted myself often. I’d heard programmers groan about debugging, but spending a whole day configuring an environment? That hit differently. Still, my drive to carve my own path pushed me past the fear. I moved from System.out.println("Hello World!"); to building two web applications from scratch.

Just 20 days ago, I wasn’t convinced I could be a software engineer. Technical interviews and complex problems felt daunting, exposing gaps I feared I couldn’t bridge. But I paused, refocused, and leaned into what I’d already learned. Optimizing my applications’ performance showed me I might just pull this off.

I’m a mix of dreamer and doer. I want skills that unlock new possibilities, rooted in what I love and believe in. Passion fuels persistence, I’ve learned. At first, coding didn’t feel like mine—I was a journalist, wired for stories, not logic. Effort, not instinct, got me here. Then, last week, a Jungian cognitive test surprised me: I’m driven by “Ti” (introverted thinking), thriving on analysis. Skeptical, I retook it twice—same result.

Curious, I asked ChatGPT about it. It wasn’t shocked—my technical questions had already hinted at a developer’s mind. But looking back, my shift to coding wasn’t random; it’s the result of persistence, curiosity, and a hunger for something bigger. Now, I believe I can make it.
